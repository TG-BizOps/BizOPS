5 Data Protection Measures — Comprehensive Overview
Encryption at RestAES-256-GCM encryption applied to all database storage volumes, backup files, and log archives. Encryption keys managed via dedicated Hardware Security Module (HSM) with automatic key rotation every 90 days.
Encryption in TransitTLS 1.3 enforced for all client-server and server-server communications. IPSec/IKEv2 VPN tunnels for inter-office data transfer. Legacy protocols (TLS 1.0/1.1, SSL) explicitly disabled.
Access ControlRole-Based Access Control (RBAC) with principle of least privilege. All administrative access requires Multi-Factor Authentication (MFA). Database access limited to named accounts with individual credentials — no shared accounts.
Data MaskingPersonal Identifiable Information (PII) is dynamically masked in non-production environments. Query-level masking applied for support staff accessing customer data. Only authorized personnel can view unmasked data with audit trail.
Backup & RecoveryDaily automated encrypted backups at 02:00 KST. Incremental backups every 6 hours. 90-day retention policy. Backups stored on geographically separated encrypted storage. Recovery Point Objective (RPO): 6 hours. Recovery Time Objective (RTO): 4 hours. Quarterly restore testing performed.
Data DeletionUpon contract termination or data subject request: Secure erasure following NIST SP 800-88 Rev.1 guidelines. Certificate of destruction issued. Physical media degaussed and shredded when decommissioned.
Audit LoggingAll access to personal data comprehensively logged: timestamp, user ID, source IP, action type, affected records. Logs are immutable (write-once storage), retained for 1 year minimum, and reviewed monthly by security team. Anomalous access patterns trigger automated alerts.
Network SecurityVLAN segmentation between application and database zones. Micro-segmentation with zero-trust network policy. All inter-zone traffic inspected by next-generation firewall. Egress filtering prevents unauthorized outbound data transfer.

2 Three-Tier Security Zone Architecture
🔓 ZONE 1 — Public Area (Lobby & Reception)

Security Level: Open Access with Monitoring

  • Access Method: Open to visitors during business hours
  • Security Guard: 24/7 on-site security personnel at reception desk
  • Visitor Registration: All visitors must sign in at reception with government-issued photo ID
  • Temporary Badge: Visitor badges issued with photo, name, date, and host employee name printed
  • CCTV: Entrance and lobby area monitored with high-definition cameras
  • Time Restriction: After-hours access requires pre-approval from building management
🔐 ZONE 2 — Office Area (Restricted Work Space)

Security Level: IC Card Authentication Required

  • Access Method: RFID IC card authentication at all entry points — card is proximity-based (no contact required)
  • Authorized Personnel: Full-time employees with active IC cards issued by HR department
  • Visitors: Must be accompanied by authorized host employee at all times — no unescorted visitor access
  • CCTV: All corridors, entry/exit points, and common areas continuously recorded
  • Clean Desk Policy: No personal information documents left on desks after work hours
  • Network Equipment: All switches, routers stored in locked cabinets within this zone
  • Screen Lock: All workstations auto-lock after 5 minutes of inactivity
🔐🔐 ZONE 3 — Server Room (Maximum Security)

Security Level: Dual-Factor Authentication (IC Card + Biometric)

  • Access Method: Two-step authentication required:
    (1) IC Card scan at outer door → (2) Fingerprint biometric scan at inner door (mantrap design)
  • Authorized Personnel: Limited to 5 named IT/Infrastructure staff — access list reviewed quarterly by CISO
  • Anti-Tailgating: Mantrap (interlocking doors) prevents unauthorized following — only one person admitted per authentication cycle
  • CCTV: Multiple camera angles covering all server racks, entry points, and maintenance areas — footage retained 90+ days
  • Environmental Controls:
    — Temperature: 20–24°C (precision CRAC units with N+1 redundancy)
    — Humidity: 40–55% RH (dehumidifiers with automatic regulation)
    — Fire Suppression: FM-200 gas-based system (safe for electronics, no water damage risk)
  • No Mobile Devices: Smartphones and cameras prohibited inside server room to prevent data photography/recording
  • Access Logging: Entry and exit timestamps electronically recorded for each individual with duration tracking

4 Access Control Policy — Detailed Specifications
IC Card IssuanceIssued by HR department upon employment. Each card uniquely encoded with employee ID, department, and authorized zone levels. Card activation requires department head + IT security team dual approval.
Biometric EnrollmentFingerprint data captured during on-boarding by IT Security team. Stored as encrypted mathematical template (not fingerprint image) on dedicated secure server. Template data deleted within 24 hours of access revocation.
Access Level Matrix Level 1 (Zone 1-2): General staff, administrative personnel
Level 2 (Zone 1-3): IT Infrastructure team, CISO (currently 5 persons)
Level 3 (All + Override): Building management emergencies only (requires 2-person authorization)
Employee TerminationIC card electronically deactivated within 1 business day of termination. Physical card collected by HR. Biometric template deleted within 24 hours. Access revocation logged and confirmed by IT Security.
Lost Card ProtocolImmediate deactivation upon report. Temporary card issued with 24-hour expiry. New card issued within 2 business days. Lost card incident logged for security audit trail.
CCTV Specifications14 HD cameras (1080p minimum) across all three zones. 24/7 continuous recording. Footage stored on encrypted NVR (Network Video Recorder) with 90+ day retention. Remote viewing by security team only. Monthly review of camera positioning and coverage.
Access Log RetentionAll electronic entry/exit events retained for 1 year minimum. Logs include: timestamp, employee ID, card number, zone, door ID, authentication result (success/failure). Monthly audit review by security administrator. Failed access attempts trigger immediate alert.
Quarterly ReviewAccess rights reviewed quarterly by CISO. Unused access rights (no entry for 90+ days) automatically flagged for review. Access list reconciled against current HR employee roster.
5 Visitor Management Procedure — Step by Step
  1. Pre-Registration (Host Employee)
    Host employee submits visitor request via internal system minimum 1 business day in advance. Request includes: visitor name, company, purpose, expected date/time, zones to be visited.
    Approval required from department head for Zone 2 access. CISO approval required for Zone 3 access.
  2. Arrival & ID Verification (Reception)
    Visitor presents government-issued photo ID at reception desk. Security guard verifies identity against pre-registration record. Photo taken for temporary badge.
    Unregistered visitors are denied entry and asked to contact their host for registration.
  3. Badge Issuance & NDA (Security Guard)
    Temporary visitor badge printed with: photo, name, company, date, host name, and authorized zones. Badge must be visibly worn at all times. Visitor signs confidentiality acknowledgment form.
    Badge has electronic deactivation — automatically expires at end of approved time window.
  4. Escorted Entry (Host Employee)
    Host employee meets visitor at reception and escorts them through IC card access points. Visitor must remain with host at all times — no independent movement within restricted zones.
    If host must temporarily leave, another authorized employee must take over escort duty.
  5. Zone 3 Access (If Required)
    Only permitted with prior CISO approval. Host + visitor both authenticated at mantrap. Visitor must surrender all electronic devices (phone, camera, USB) to secure locker before entry.
    All activities in Zone 3 during visitor presence are recorded in detail.
  6. Exit & Badge Return (Reception)
    Visitor returns temporary badge to reception. Exit time recorded in visitor log. Host confirms visitor departure. Badge electronically deactivated and physically collected.
    Unreturned badges trigger security alert. Badge access immediately revoked if not returned within 1 hour of scheduled departure.
6 Physical Security Incident Response

⚠️ Defined Response Procedures for Physical Security Events

  • Unauthorized Access Attempt: IC card reader logs failed attempt → automated alert to security team → guard dispatched within 2 minutes → incident documented and reviewed
  • Tailgating Detection: CCTV AI analytics flag suspected tailgating → security guard verification → if confirmed, escort out and incident report filed
  • Lost/Stolen IC Card: Immediate electronic deactivation → security patrol of affected zone → review CCTV for unauthorized use → replace card with new credentials
  • After-Hours Anomaly: Access attempts outside business hours flagged for review → security guard visual confirmation → management notification if unauthorized
  • Natural Disaster/Emergency: Emergency exit routes clearly marked → manual door release for evacuation → post-event security sweep → access system integrity verification

• Behavioral AI: Runtime monitoring — detects anomalous process behavior, lateral movement, living-off-the-land techniques even for never-before-seen threats
Core Capabilities • Prevention: Pre-execution blocking of known and unknown malware
• Detection: Real-time behavioral analysis, fileless attack detection, memory-only threat detection
• Response: Automated containment, process termination, network isolation (1-click)
• Hunting: Deep Visibility — full endpoint telemetry with 14-day queryable data retention for proactive threat hunting
• Forensics: Attack storyline visualization, process tree reconstruction, IOC extraction
• Remediation: One-click rollback to pre-attack state using Volume Shadow Copy; surgical file removal without full system reimage
• Automation: STAR (SentinelOne Threat-Aware Response) custom rules for automated actions

3 Alert Response Procedure — Detailed Incident Workflow
  1. Detection & Automated Response (0–1 minute)
    SentinelOne AI engine detects anomalous behavior pattern. Automated response activates immediately:
    • Malicious process killed and quarantined
    • If ransomware detected: automatic network isolation of endpoint
    • Alert generated with full context: process tree, file hash, network connections, MITRE ATT&CK technique mapping
    • High/Critical severity triggers immediate push notification + SMS to on-call security team (24/7 rotation)
  2. Triage & Initial Assessment (Critical: 15 min / High: 1 hr)
    On-call security analyst receives alert and begins triage:
    • Review SentinelOne Attack Storyline — visual kill chain showing complete attack flow
    • Assess threat severity: Is data at risk? Is there lateral movement? Is this a known threat or novel?
    • Query Deep Visibility for related indicators across all endpoints (scope assessment)
    • Determine if additional endpoints need immediate isolation
    • Classify incident per internal severity matrix
  3. Containment & Isolation (Immediate upon confirmation)
    Confirmed threat triggers full containment protocol:
    • Network Isolation: One-click endpoint isolation via SentinelOne console (endpoint can only communicate with S1 cloud)
    • Process Termination: All related malicious processes killed across affected endpoints
    • IOC Blocking: Malicious file hashes, IPs, domains added to block list across all endpoints organization-wide
    • Account Lockdown: If credential theft suspected, affected user accounts temporarily disabled pending investigation
    • Notification: CISO and IT management notified. If personal data potentially affected, Data Protection Officer (DPO) notified.
  4. Deep Forensic Investigation (2–8 hours)
    Comprehensive forensic analysis conducted:
    • Attack Vector: Determine initial entry point (phishing, exploit, supply chain, insider)
    • Lateral Movement: Trace any movement to other systems using Deep Visibility query results
    • Data Impact: Assess if any personal data was accessed, exfiltrated, or encrypted
    • Timeline: Reconstruct complete attack timeline from initial compromise to detection
    • IOC Extraction: Collect all Indicators of Compromise for threat intelligence sharing
    • Evidence Preservation: Forensic images captured for potential legal proceedings
  5. Remediation & System Recovery (4–24 hours)
    Systematic cleanup and restoration:
    • Malware Removal: SentinelOne surgical remediation removes all malicious artifacts without full reimage
    • Rollback: If files damaged/encrypted, SentinelOne Rollback restores to pre-attack state using VSS snapshots
    • Patch Application: If vulnerability exploited, emergency patch applied to all affected systems
    • Credential Reset: All potentially compromised credentials rotated
    • Detection Rule Update: New STAR rules deployed to detect any recurrence of this specific attack pattern
    • Verification: Full scan of remediated endpoints to confirm clean state before network reconnection
  6. Post-Incident Review & Reporting (Within 5 business days)
    Comprehensive post-incident process:
    • Incident Report: Formal documentation including: executive summary, timeline, root cause, impact assessment, actions taken, recommendations
    • Lessons Learned: Team review meeting to identify process improvements
    • Policy Updates: Security policies and procedures updated based on findings
    • Detection Tuning: SentinelOne rules and policies refined to improve future detection
    • Regulatory Notification: If personal data breach confirmed, notification procedures initiated per APPI / PIPA requirements (within 72 hours of confirmation)
    • Stakeholder Communication: Summary briefing to management and affected parties as appropriate

5 Proactive Threat Hunting & Continuous Improvement

🔍 Beyond Reactive — Proactive Security Measures

  • Weekly Threat Hunting: Security team conducts proactive threat hunting using SentinelOne Deep Visibility — querying 14 days of endpoint telemetry for IOCs published by threat intelligence feeds (CISA, JP-CERT, KrCERT)
  • Monthly MITRE ATT&CK Review: Detection coverage mapped against MITRE ATT&CK framework. Gaps identified and addressed with new STAR detection rules or additional security controls.
  • Quarterly Tabletop Exercise: Simulated incident response exercise conducted quarterly. Scenarios include ransomware, data exfiltration, and insider threat. Results used to refine response procedures and identify training needs.
  • Threat Intelligence Integration: SentinelOne Threat Intelligence feed automatically enriches all detections with global context — known malware families, threat actor attribution, geographic targeting patterns.
  • Vendor Collaboration: Regular engagement with SentinelOne technical account manager for product updates, emerging threat briefings, and configuration optimization recommendations.
